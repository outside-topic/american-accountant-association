Academic Collaboration

Elevating Excellence Through Strategic Partnerships

The Institutional Affiliation Program of the American Association of Accountants (AAA) is designed to recognize, support, and collaborate with educational institutions, professional training providers, corporate academies, and industry organizations that align with AAA’s professional standards, competency frameworks, and ethical governance principles.

Through institutional affiliation, AAA establishes a structured partnership framework that enables organizations to align their academic programs, professional training, curriculum standards, assessment methodologies, and faculty development practices with internationally benchmarked accounting and finance competencies. AAA affiliation enhances institutional credibility, strengthens graduate employability, promotes professional alignment, and fosters global recognition

"Affiliated institutions become part of a professional ecosystem committed to excellence, integrity, innovation, and lifelong learning."

What is Institutional Affiliation?

Institutional Affiliation is a formal partnership agreement between AAA and an eligible organization whereby the institution aligns its programs, training delivery, assessment practices, and quality assurance mechanisms with AAA’s professional standards and competency frameworks.

Note: Affiliation does not replace national accreditation or licensing. Instead, it serves as a professional alignment and quality endorsement demonstrating commitment to globally relevant professional standards.

Step-by-Step

The Affiliation Journey

AAA follows a structured, transparent, and quality-driven affiliation process to ensure mutual excellence.

Step 1: Expression of Interest (EOI)

Submission of organizational profile, program portfolio, accreditation detials and strategic objectives.

01
Step 2: Eligibility Screening

AAA reviews legal status, governance structure, academic credibility, and ethical compliance.

02
Step 3: Self-Assessment & Documentation

Institution completes a self-assessment covering of curriculum alignment, faculty qualifications, assessment methods, quality assurance systems and facilities and learning resources.

03
Step 4: Evaluation & Review

AAA conducts : desktop reviews, Virtual or onsite assessment, interviews with management and faculty ,evidence verification.

04
Step 5: Affiliation Decision

Affiliation Committee issues: approval, conditional approval , improvement plan (if required).

05
Step 6: Agreement & Onboarding
Formal agreement covering:
  • Branding usage & Compliance obligations
  • Reporting requirements and renewal terms
06
Step 7: Continuous Monitoring & Renewal

Annual reporting, periodic reviews, Quality audits and Continuous improvement feedback.

07

Affiliation Quality Standards

Affiliation Responsibilities

Institutional partners agree to actively uphold their side of the collaboration through:

  • Maintaining alignment with AAA standards
  • Use branding responsibly
  • Submit annual reports
  • Participate in audits
  • Promote ethical conduct
