Foundational Commitment

Integrity and Public Trust

The American Association of Accountants (AAA) is committed to upholding the highest standards of professional competence, ethical conduct, and public trust. Our Ethics and Professional Standards framework ensures that all members operate with integrity, accountability, transparency, and professional responsibility.

AAA recognizes that finance & accounting professionals serve a critical role in protecting stakeholders, investors, organizations, regulators, and society. Members are expected to maintain not only technical excellence but also ethical judgment, independence, and professional discipline.

All members are required to comply with AAA’s Professional Standards, Code of Ethics, and CPD policy as a condition of membership.

Professional Standards

Purpose of Standards

Establishing Excellence

AAA Professional Standards establish consistent benchmarks for professional competence, performance quality, accountability, and professional conduct. These standards guide how members perform their professional duties and how professional quality is maintained across the profession.

The standards ensure that AAA members:
  • Deliver high-quality professional services
  • Apply recognized technical frameworks
  • Maintain professional independence and objectivity
  • Protect confidentiality and data security
  • Demonstrate accountability and transparency
  • Uphold public interest responsibilities
Applicability

Financial analytics and technology
Academia and research
Core Framework

Core Professional Standards

1. Technical Competence
  • Maintain up-to-date knowledge of applicable accounting standards (e.g., IFRS, GAAP)
  • Apply professional judgment appropriately
  • Ensure accuracy, completeness, and reliability of financial information
  • Perform work within their professional competence
2. Quality & Professional Diligence
  • Exercise due care and professional skepticism
  • Follow documented methodologies and best practices
  • Maintain adequate documentation and audit trails
  • Meet deadlines and contractual obligations
3. Independence & Objectivity
  • Avoid conflicts of interest
  • Disclose potential conflicts transparently
  • Maintain impartiality in professional judgment
  • Protect independence in assurance engagements
4. Confidentiality & Data Protection
  • Safeguard confidential information
  • Comply with data protection regulations
  • Prevent unauthorized disclosure or misuse
  • Apply secure information handling practices
5. Regulatory Compliance
  • Comply with applicable laws and regulations
  • Maintain proper licensing where required
  • Follow organizational policies and professional requirements
6. Professional Accountability
  • Accept responsibility for professional decisions
  • Cooperate with professional reviews and investigations
  • Maintain transparent professional records
Foundational Values

Professional Ethics

Commitment to Ethical Excellence: AAA members are bound by a comprehensive Code of Professional Ethics designed to protect the public interest, promote professional integrity, and ensure responsible professional conduct.

Ethics is not optional — it is fundamental to professional credibility.
1. Integrity

Members shall act honestly, fairly, and truthfully in all professional activities.

2. Objectivity

Members shall avoid bias, conflicts of interest, or undue influence.

3. Professional Competence

Members shall maintain adequate knowledge and skills and perform duties competently.

4. Confidentiality

Members shall respect confidential information and protect sensitive data.

5. Professional Behavior

Members shall comply with laws, regulations, and professional obligations.

6. Public Interest Responsibility

Members shall act in ways that protect stakeholders and societal trust.

Ethical Decision-Making Framework

AAA encourages members to evaluate ethical situations using the following approach:

1
Identify ethical risks and stakeholders
2
Assess applicable standards and regulations
3
Evaluate alternatives and consequences
4
Seek guidance where necessary
5
Document decisions and rationale

Disciplinary Governance

AAA maintains an independent Ethics & Disciplinary Committee that investigates complaints, conducts fair hearings, and applies proportionate disciplinary actions, ensures transparency and due process . Possible actions include advisory warnings, mandatory training, suspension, or termination of membership.

Career Development

Continuing Professional Development (CPD)

CPD ensures members maintain current technical competence, professional relevance, and ethical awareness in a rapidly changing profession. AAA requires all members to complete annual CPD to maintain active membership status.

Annual CPD Requirements

Membership Level Minimum Annual CPD

Professional Member 30 hours
Senior Professional Member 40 hours
